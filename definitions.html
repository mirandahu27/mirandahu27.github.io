Terminology and Definitions

Heteronormativity

adjective

Denoting or relating to a world view that promotes heterosexuality as the normal or preferred sexual orientation.

Gender

Gender Identity

noun

An individual's personal sense of having a particular gender.

Agender

adjective

Denoting or relating to a person who does not identify themselves as having a particular gender.

Androgynous

adjective

Partly male and partly female in appearance; of indeterminate sex.

Bigender

adjective

Denoting or relating to a person whose sense of personal identity encompasses two genders.

Cisgender

adjective

Denoting or relating to a person whose sense of personal identity and gender corresponds with their birth sex.

Transgender

adjective

Denoting or relating to a person whose sense of personal identity and gender does not correspond with their birth sex.

Gender Fluid

adjective

Denoting or relating to a person who does not identify themselves as having a fixed gender.

Non-Binary

adjective

Denoting or relating to a gender or sexual identity that is not defined in terms of traditional binary oppositions such as male and female or homosexual and heterosexual.

Genderqueer

noun

A person who does not subscribe to conventional gender distinctions but identifies with neither, both, or a combination of male and female genders.

adjective

Denoting or relating to a person who does not subscribe to conventional gender distinctions but identifies with neither, both, or a combination of male and female genders.

Intersex

adjective

Individuals born with any of several sex characteristics including chromosome patterns, gonads, or genitals that, according to the Office of the United Nations High Commissioner for Human Rights, "do not fit typical binary notions of male or female bodies".

Two-Spirit

adjective

Native people in their communities who fulfill a traditional third-gender ceremonial and social role in their cultures.

Sexuality

Sexual Orientation

noun

A person's identity in relation to the gender or genders to which they are sexually attracted; the fact of being heterosexual, homosexual, etc.

Asexual

adjective

Not involving sexual activity, feelings, or associations; nonsexual.

Bisexual

adjective

Sexually attracted not exclusively to people of one particular gender; attracted to both men and women.

Demisexual

adjective

People who only feel sexually attracted to someone when they have an emotional bond with the person.

Heterosexual

adjective

(Of a person) Sexually attracted to people of the opposite sex.

Homosexual

adjective

(Of a person) Sexually attracted to people of one's own sex.

Pansexual

adjective

Not limited in sexual choice with regard to biological sex, gender, or gender identity.
